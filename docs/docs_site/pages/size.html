Overview

Sample size calculation and allocation is usually one of the first steps in conducting a survey. Sample size calculations allow the survey implementers to collect enough data to achieve targeted precision levels. To do so, the sample size calculation should follow the actual sampling design as closely as possible and take into account imperfections of survey implementations such as nonresponse. Since sample sizes are calculated prior to implementing the survey, many of the input data needed to calculate sample sizes are obtained from previous experience and from reasonable assumptions.

In this tutorial, we illustrate the calculation and allocation of some common sample size methods. Users can find additional details from Chow et al. (2018) and Ryan (2013).
